Explore this record using LightWAVE

LightWAVE is a lightweight waveform and annotation viewer and editor. Use it to explore the record named in the upper right corner. Use your web browser's Back button to return to the previous page when you're finished.

On the View/edit tab, you can see a 10-second excerpt of the record named in the upper right corner. The time indicators along the lower edge of the plot indicate the elapsed time from the beginning of the record, in hours, minutes, and seconds.

View any other location in the record using the navigation controls along the top of the View/edit tab. For example, enter a time in the box next to Go to:, then click (tap) Plot.

To find out how any LightWAVE control works, move the pointer over it, and a tooltip with a brief description will appear after a short interval.

Click on any of the labels on the left side of the View/edit tab to remove that item from the plot. Click again to restore it. This feature may be useful if there are many signals or annotations, or if you have an unusually slow network connection.

If you have a mouse with a scroll wheel or a multitouch trackpad or screen, point to any location in the plot and scroll or "pinch" to change the magnification.

View details about the record, including text representations of the annotations and digitized signals, on the Tables tab.

When your browser loads this page, not all of LightWAVE's controls are available; for example, you cannot select a different record to view. To access all of LightWAVE's features, visit http://physionet.org/lightwave/. Click on Help topics above to learn more about LightWAVE.
